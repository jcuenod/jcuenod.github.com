    About Me

    My name is James Cuénod, I'm a millenial so I guess that means technology is par for the course. I started programming when I was barely a teenager though (which is overzealous if you want friends in highschool - but I discovered that too late).

    Now coding is a way of life and I don't know how I would survive if I couldn't.

    I just learn whatever I think looks interesting. I started with QBasic, progressed through Visual Basic to Java and have had some fun with Android. Along the way I picked up HTML and CSS (when it was still busy revolutionising table layouts) and Javascript. JQuery changed my life and although I had fun with PHP (and Drupal and then Wordpress), Rails threatens to undo my appreciation for PHP.

    How coding fits into my understanding of life the universe and everything is an interesting question. It's really a theological question because theology is where you find those sorts of answers (unless you want "42," in which case grab a towel). I think of my theological answer, in non-theological language, in terms that Tolkien puts better than I do.

    In his poem Mythopoiea, Tolkien speaks of the mistake humanity (the lords of creation) makes when we worship what we make but how making things is still a critical aspect of being human.

    Dis-graced he may be, yet is not dethroned,
    and keeps the rags of lordship once he owned,
    his world-dominion by creative act:
    not his to worship the great Artefact,
    Man, Sub-creator, the refracted light
    through whom is splintered from a single White
    to many hues, and endlessly combined
    in living shapes that move from mind to mind.
